Ajiaco

Description

Ajiaco, a beloved Colombian soup, is a celebration of hearty flavors. Combining three varieties of potatoes, chicken, and corn simmered with guascas, the broth achieves a rich and distinctive taste. Garnished with avocado, capers, and a dollop of sour cream, ajiaco is a comforting dish that embodies the warmth and richness of Colombian culinary heritage, creating a delightful experience for those who come together to enjoy.

In preparing ajiaco, the simmering process melds the flavors of the ingredients, and the final touch involves artfully garnishing each bowl. The result is a visually appealing and flavor-packed soup that not only satisfies the palate but also brings people closer through a shared appreciation for the cultural essence of Colombian cuisine.

Steps

  1. Prepare ingredients

    Gather chicken, potatoes (including papas criollas), corn on the cob, guascas, onions, garlic, cilantro, chicken broth or water, salt, pepper, avocado, capers, and sour cream.

  2. Simmer Chicken and Vegetables

    In a large pot, simmer chicken, potatoes, corn, onions, garlic, and guascas in chicken broth or water until the chicken is cooked and the potatoes are tender.

  3. Add Cilantro and Season

    Stir in chopped cilantro and season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the flavors to meld during simmering.

  4. Garnish

    Serve the Ajiaco hot. Garnish each bowl with slices of avocado, a sprinkle of capers, and a dollop of sour cream.

  5. Enjoy

    Dive into the rich and flavorful Ajiaco, savoring the unique combination of ingredients and the cultural essence of Colombian cuisine.
